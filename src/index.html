Introduction

Welcome to Prague! This city, known as "the city of a hundred spires", is the capital of the Czech Republic. Prague's architecture is a great testament to its richness and beauty. It is known for being the center of the country's rich history thanks to its magnificent baroque buildings, medieval Astronomical Clock and Gothic churches.

12 Interesting facts

  1. Charles Bridge is one of the most famous landmarks in Prague. Charles IV laid the foundation stone of this famous monument at 5:31 am on July 9, 1357 due to his superstition with numerology.
  2. Prague Castle is the largest castle in the world, with more than 700 rooms.
  3. The Golden Alley in Prague Castle is a very curious place. In its colorful little houses lived first the permanent guard of the castle and then goldsmiths craftsmen.
  4. The Czech Republic is the country where the most beer is drunk per capita.
  5. The Rolling Stones paid for the lighting system of Prague Castle.
  6. John Lennon is very present in Prague although he was never there.
  7. The historic center of Prague was not bombed during World War II, so the monuments and buildings we see are the original ones.
  8. Prague is one of the 20 most visited cities in the world.
  9. The Old Town Square contains several curiosities of Prague, including the decoration of the facade of some of its buildings.
  10. Prague is known as the city of 100 towers.
  11. The Prague Zoo sells "elephant urine", which is actually a herbal liqueur.
  12. The city was founded by the duchess and prophetess Libuše and her husband, Přemysl, in the 8th century according to a legend.
